# Does Influencer Marketing Hurt Long-Term Customer Loyalty?
### A Retail Strategy Case Study | Sharvee Shrotriya

---

## The Business Question

Brands are spending billions on synchronized influencer campaigns — 
coordinated drops across multiple creators to maximize reach and create 
buying urgency. It works. Sales spike.

But what happens after the spike?

This project investigates whether synchronized influencer marketing 
creates short-term revenue gains at the cost of long-term customer 
loyalty — and what that means for retail strategy.

---

## Hypothesis

Synchronized influencer campaigns generate measurable short-term 
revenue spikes, but post-campaign periods show:
- Lower repeat purchase rates
- Higher return rates
- Declining average order value

If true, brands optimizing for campaign ROI may be systematically 
underinvesting in loyalty — and overestimating customer lifetime value.

---

## Approach

This is a retail strategy case study backed by data — not a pure 
data science project. The goal is business insight, not model complexity.

**Phase 1 — Data Foundation**
- Dataset: UCI Online Retail II (500k+ transactions, UK retailer)
- Campaign proxy: Google Trends spikes as influencer activity signals
- Tool: SQL for data cleaning and cohort analysis
